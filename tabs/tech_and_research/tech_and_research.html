Research Pillars

01

Autonomous Navigation in Unstructured Environments

Developing algorithms for robots to traverse complex, unpredictable terrains without prior mapping or human guidance. Our research addresses challenges such as obstacle avoidance, path planning, and real-time environment perception in disaster zones.

02

Medical AI for Remote Triage

Creating AI systems capable of accurately assessing injury severity and performing triage decisions comparable to trained medical professionals. This research combines computer vision, machine learning, and medical expertise to analyze visual and sensor data for injury detection.

03

Multi-Robot Coordination Strategies

Investigating optimal strategies for teams of heterogeneous robots to collaborate in time-critical scenarios. Our research focuses on distributed decision-making, task allocation, and coordination protocols for maximizing coverage and efficiency.

04

Human-Robot Teaming for Emergency Response

Exploring effective methods for robots and human responders to work as an integrated team. This research addresses interface design, communication protocols, and trust-building to enable productive collaboration between AI systems and medical personnel.

Recent Publications

March 2025 IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters

Non-Contact Vital Sign Monitoring for Autonomous Triage Systems

J. Chen, A. Rodriguez, S. Kim, M. Williams

This paper presents a novel approach to remote vital sign detection using a combination of thermal imaging and mmWave radar technology, enabling accurate heart rate and respiration monitoring from distances up to 3 meters.

Remote Sensing Vital Signs Medical Robotics
January 2025 Conference on Artificial Intelligence in Medicine (AIME)

Deep Learning for Trauma Assessment from Visual Data in Emergency Scenarios

L. Thompson, N. Patel, R. Garcia, T. Wilson

We introduce a multi-modal neural network architecture that combines RGB, depth, and thermal imagery to detect and classify common traumatic injuries with accuracy comparable to emergency medical technicians.

Computer Vision Trauma Detection Medical AI
November 2024 Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS)

Distributed Task Allocation for Heterogeneous Robot Teams in Time-Critical Triage Operations

H. Nguyen, K. Singh, M. Johnson, A. Lee

This paper presents an algorithm for optimal task allocation among heterogeneous robot teams, accounting for different robot capabilities, remaining battery life, and casualty priority in time-critical scenarios.
